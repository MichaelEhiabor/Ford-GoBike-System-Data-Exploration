Part II - Ford GoBike System Data Exploration¶

by Michael Utomi Ehiabor¶

Investigation Overview¶

I wanted to have a look at the time of the day and day of the week that most trips were taken, how long does it take to complete an average trip and see if the results of the ealier investigations depend on if a user is a subscriber or a customer.

Dataset Overview¶

After data cleaning and preparation for exploration, the dataset contains 183,412 rows and 11 columns:

  • duration_sec
  • start_time
  • start_station_name
  • end_station_name
  • User_type
  • member_birth_year
  • member_gender
  • start_hour
  • day_time
  • day_of_week
  • user_age

Highest Number of Trips Taken by Day of The Week¶

Thursday is the day with the highest number of trips taken followed by Tuesday and Wednesday. Saturday and Sunday are the days with the least number of trips, this might be due to the fact that Subscribers use the ride mostly for going to work or school

Highest Number of Trips Taken by Time of The Day¶

The time of the day with the most trips is in the morning with 70,465 (38.4%) trips taken while night has the lowest with 15605 (8.5%) trips taken. This is expected because of the number of people who are heading to work or have places to be.

Duration Distribution¶

After standardizing by converting from seconds to minutes and then plotting on a logarithmic scale, the distribution of trip duration takes on a unimodal shape and shows that most trips taken lasted between 4 to 20 minutes

Average trip duration by user type¶

Customers (23.9 minutes) spent more time on trips on average than Subscribers (10.7 minutes). This might be because Subscribers mostly used the rides for short everyday comutes like going from home to work or to school

Number Of Rides On Days Of The Week By User Type¶

we can see that the day of the week that the highest number of trips (Thursday) is not dependent on the user type. The graph also shows that there is a reduction in the number of rides taken by Subscribers on Saturdays and Sundays, this is understandable because a lot of subscribers do not have to go to work during the weekends. As for casaul riders, there is not major change in the number of rides taken on different days of the week.

Summary¶

  • The number of Subscriber trips is significantly Higher than that of Customer
  • Customers (23.9 minutes) spent more time on trips on average than Subscribers (10.7 minutes). This might be because Subscribers mostly used the rides for short everyday comutes like going from home to work or to school
  • There is a reduction in the number of rides taken by Subscribers on Saturdays and Sundays, this is understandable because a lot of subscribers do not have to go to work during the weekends. As for casaul riders, there is no major change in the number of rides taken on different days of the week.
  • Customers had longer rides on average than Subscribers for every day of the week with Sundays and Saturdays being signifantly higher than the rest of the week days
  • The Average trip duration for each time of the day for the Subscribers is relatively constant with slight increase in the afternoon while for Customers the average trip duration increases significantly in the afternoon and at night.
